Abstract

Do video foundation models trained on Western data understand the dense, crowded, chaotic streets of India? We introduce DenseWorld-200K, a large-scale benchmark of 200,000 video clips from 714 YouTube walking tours across 25+ Indian cities. Each clip is automatically tagged with 16 structured attributes (scene type, crowd density, traffic mix, road surface, etc.) using a VLM bake-off won by Qwen3-VL-8B.

We pivot from spatial scene retrieval to motion-class probing: each clip's 23-D camera-subtracted optical-flow signature (RAFT, foreground residual, Phase 0 / iter15) is binned into 8 classes — magnitude (still / slow / medium / fast) × dominant foreground direction (rightward / upward / leftward / downward). We evaluate seven encoders — V-JEPA 2.1 (ViT-G, frozen), V-JEPA Pretrain (continual SSL, λ=1e-4), V-JEPA Surgery (recipe-v3 progressive prefix unfreeze, 3stage_DI + noDI variants), DINOv2, CLIP, and a Random baseline. Our key finding: V-JEPA dominates motion-class top-1 (0.84 frozen baseline), validating that temporal encoders capture agent motion — the dimension where image encoders have no signal. Surgery (R1 winner) lifts top-1 to 0.846; iter15 head-only freeze targets the same gain at 1/40× the GPU cost.

Key Findings

V-JEPA 0.84 vs DINOv2 ~0.55 top-1
Video Beats Image (on motion). When the task is motion-class prediction (8 RAFT-derived classes, camera-subtracted foreground flow), V-JEPA's temporal encoder dominates the image encoders. The retrieval-vs-motion gap is what makes V-JEPA fit-for-purpose on Indian street dynamics.
Surgery R1: 0.846 — +1.5 pp over frozen
Recipe-v3 Surgery Wins. iter14 paired-Δ sweep: R1 (SALT + LP-FT + Surgical Fine-Tuning + SPD + CLEAR + MGMAE) lifts motion top-1 from 0.835 (frozen anchor) to 0.846. R3, R5, R6 are load-bearing — dropping any of them regresses to 0.823. 95% paired BCa CI confirms non-overlap.
Surgery costs 96 GB / 8 GPU-hr; Head-only fits 24 GB / 8 hr
Head-Only Freeze: 1/40× the GPU Bill. iter15 hypothesis: a 432K-param motion_aux head trained on FROZEN V-JEPA encoder + FROZEN predictor matches surgery's top-1 lift. No backward through 1.84 B encoder params → no activation storage → ViT-G fits 24 GB RTX Pro 4000 at $0.20/hr.
Adapted 0.838 vs Frozen 0.835 — Δ ≈ +0.3 pp
10K Clips Can't Move a 1B Model. Continual pretraining (V-JEPA JEPA + motion_aux, λ=1e-4, 5 epochs) on the 10K POC subset produces near-noise improvement. The full 115K corpus is the unblocker; this POC validated the pipeline, not the encoder.

Why Motion-Class, Not Scene Retrieval

The retrieval framing was the wrong frame.

V-JEPA 2.1 is trained to predict masked spatiotemporal patches — it learns motion patterns, camera trajectories, and temporal dynamics. An earlier evaluation framed the task as spatial scene retrieval (kNN over a 16-axis scene-attribute taxonomy) and found V-JEPA underperforming image encoders. That gap was real, but it measured V-JEPA on the axis where it has no comparative advantage.

The right framing: motion-class probing. Each clip's 23-D camera-subtracted optical-flow signature (RAFT, foreground-only after subtracting per-pair median camera motion) is binned into 8 classes — magnitude (still / slow / medium / fast) × dominant foreground direction. This taxonomy isolates agent motion from camera motion, the exact decomposition V-JEPA's temporal encoder is built to produce. Result: V-JEPA frozen top-1 reaches 0.84; surgery R1 lifts it to 0.846; image encoders cap around 0.55.

External validation: arXiv:2509.21595 "Temporal vs Spatial: Comparing DINOv3 and V-JEPA2" reports the same spatial/temporal trade-off on UCF Sports — image encoders win on appearance, video encoders win on dynamics. We confirm the same dichotomy on the dense, cluttered, camera-handheld Indian street regime where the gap is largest.

iter15 next step: head-only freeze + curriculum. If a 432K-param head on a frozen ViT-G matches surgery's top-1 lift, the paper claim becomes "motion-class transfer is a head-level adaptation problem — the encoder already has the right features; you just have to read them." That single sentence shifts the field from "fine-tune the whole encoder" to "freeze the encoder, train the read-out".
